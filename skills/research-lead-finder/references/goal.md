## The goal

Surface specific, checkable mechanistic connections between recent artificial-life
and open-endedness work that bear on one question: what structural conditions keep a
search process from stagnating, and where do different formalisms (novelty search,
quality-diversity, minimal-criterion coevolution, autocurricula) share a failure mode
rather than just sharing vocabulary.

## Passes the floor

A candidate passes only if it could plausibly support a lead that is all of:

- About a mechanism or structural condition, not a topic or a result in isolation.
- Connected to the goal question above, not merely in the same subfield.
- Specific enough that someone could later check whether the connection holds.

## Fails the floor

Discard candidates that are only:

- A single paper with no plausible join to another retrieved item.
- On-topic by area but with nothing mechanistic to say ("this is also about
  open-endedness" is not enough).
- A restatement of a paper's own abstract with no cross-paper tension or connection.

## Notes for the run

- The floor is pass/fail, not a ranking. Passing the floor does not make a lead good.
- It is correct for a run to discard most candidates here. A narrow floor is working
  as intended.
